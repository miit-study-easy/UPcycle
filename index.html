About SNPP

Small Nuclear Power Plants (SNPP)

The primary reasons for building small nuclear power plants are

  • Enhanced Safety
  • Lower Capital Costs
  • Flexibility and Scalability
  • Environmental Impact

  • Enhanced Saftey

    SMRs incorporate advanced safety features and designs that reduce the risk of accidents. Their smaller size means less radioactive material is involved, and many are designed with passive safety systems that require no human intervention to shut down safely in an emergency.

  • Lower Capital Costs

    Building smaller reactors requires less initial investment compared to large nuclear power plants. This lower financial barrier makes nuclear energy more accessible and reduces the economic risk for investors.

  • Flexibility and Scalability

    SMRs can be built in stages, allowing for gradual increases in power capacity. This modular approach enables energy providers to align the plant's output with growing demand, reducing the risk of overbuilding.
